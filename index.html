Find My Coffee

The Iron Yard | Education | 2017


For this project, students were tasked with designing one feature of a mobile app for a coffee retailer, using either a prexisting or fictional brand. I chose to design for Counter Culture Coffee, a local roaster and distributor from Durham, NC, with a national retail network.


We began the process researching brand character, target audience and creating a SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) analysis of the product/company. It's clear that the company is driven by a creative and meticulous approach to coffee making. Their attention to detail and refined packaging are reflections of their brand ethos, dedication to quality, innovation and sustainability.


Where I felt there was room for improvement was in their ability to attract new customers, specifically those without a preexisting palette who would have difficulty distinguishing between the vast array of flavors and types of coffee offered. In attempts to solve this issue, I imagined the Find My Coffee app, which helps customers decide which coffee to buy based on their taste preferences. By answering a series of easy, yes-or-no questions the customer is able to hone in on a variety of coffee that suits them, without feeling put off by overwhelming options. Additionally, the bright colors used in the design serve to reinforce brand style and provide a fun user experience.


With a concept in place, I began to develop my idea through sketches, wireframes and static mockups, which were later converted into a semi-functional, clickable prototype demonstrating the flow of the app. Spanning a gamut of tools/resources, from pen and paper to Sketch and Invision, this project was a crash course in process-driven design.

Third Uncle Records

Freelance | 2016


Based out of Richmond, IN, Third Uncle Records has released multiple projects from No One Mind in 2016. Serving dual roles as visual artist and musician, I've had the opportunity to craft design and illustration that closely match the sonic and emotional aesthetic of the music.

Elsewhere Museum

Freelance | 2016


Elsewhere Museum is an artist residency and self-described "Living Museum" set within a former thrift store, in downtown Greensboro, NC. Elsewhere invites artists from around the world to create work from the "collection," reusing and reinterpreting the materials. I've had the honor to work with the team to design their 2016 Museum/Residency Brochure and Annual Fundraising Posters in 2014 and 2016.


For the brochure, the design process began with an initial sitdown with the Director of the museum to discuss overall concepts, direction and visual aesthetic, as well as to establish a timeline of deliverables.
